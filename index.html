Construction

Pre-migration questions for QuickBooks features, migration paths, and strategic importance.

1
Do you use QuickBooks Time or Desktop timesheets for labor costing?
↳ Layer 2: Do you track billable mileage to pass on to clients?
▾
📋 QuickBooks Feature & Migration Details
✗ Does Not Move

Timesheets & Mileage: Timesheet data, Pay Item Mappings, and settings do not move. Previous mileage data does not transfer.

⚠️ Why It Matters

Labor is often the largest construction cost. Knowing this prepares the business to manually add historical mileage and completely re-establish time-tracking connections on Day 1.

2
Do you rely on Job Statuses, Start Dates, or End Dates to manage active projects?
↳ Layer 2: Do you categorize projects by specific "Job Types"?
▾
📋 QuickBooks Feature & Migration Details
⚠ Partially Moves

Job Metadata: Jobs migrate as Projects, but metadata — Job Status, Start Date, Projected End, End Date, Job Description, and Job Type — do not move over.

⚠️ Why It Matters

Project managers will lose this metadata during the switch. They must be prepared to manually recreate these tracking details to keep project timelines accurate.

3
How do you manage your subcontractors?
↳ Layer 2: Do you use Vendor Custom Fields to track insurance expirations or license numbers?
▾
📋 QuickBooks Feature & Migration Details
⚠ Partially Moves

1099 Vendors & Custom Fields: 1099 Contractors move over, but Vendor Custom Fields, Alt Contacts, and Vendor Types do not move.

⚠️ Why It Matters

Construction relies heavily on compliance. Losing subcontractor custom fields means they could accidentally hire an uninsured sub on Day 1 if they don't manually rebuild that data post-migration.

4
Do you need AIA-style billing?
↳ Layer 2: Are you required to break your Sales Tax by line items on Estimates/Invoices?
▾
📋 QuickBooks Feature & Migration Details
✗ Not Available in IES Natively

Sales Tax by Line Items: IES cannot display Sales Tax at the line-item level. Customers requiring this need a third-party AIA Billing application.

⚠️ Why It Matters

Construction companies break down sales tax by line item in AIA billing to apply correct tax rates to different materials and services, ensure compliance, and provide auditable cost details.

5
For the inventory you have, are you tracking the basic Quantity in and Quantity out?
↳ Layer 2: Do you have specific Units of Measure you track (by foot, weight, etc.)?
▾
📋 QuickBooks Feature & Migration Details
✗ Does Not Exist Natively

Multiple Units of Measure: The ability to track specific units of measure does not exist natively in the IES ecosystem without a third-party app.

⚠️ Why It Matters

Critical inventory tracking by unit of measure will convert to standard quantity in/out. Uncovering this early ensures a third-party inventory app is scoped and integrated before migration.

6
Do you pay sales tax to multiple jurisdictions or states? All Industries
↳ Layer 2: How frequently do you file your sales tax payments?
▾
📋 QuickBooks Feature & Migration Details
✗ Migrates Incorrectly

Sales Tax: Sales tax payments migrate as journal entries and often apply to the wrong filings in the online system.

⚠️ Why It Matters

To avoid compliance issues and duplicate tax payments, the accounting team must delete migrated sales tax payments and recreate them in the new Sales Tax Center.

Construction: What Migrates
The reality of construction data migration — automated vs. manual rebuilds.
🏗️
The Executive RealityMoving data is an active construction project. The core structure holds, but certain custom data and metadata will not survive the transfer. Identify site hazards before Day 1.

✅ Automated Migration (Crosses Safely)

  • Base Job Profiles (Jobs → Projects)
  • 1099 Contractor Names
  • Bank Feed Logic (must be reconnected)

🔨 Day 1 Rebuild Bucket (Drops — Requires Manual Rebuild)

  • Custom Vendor Fields
  • Job Metadata (Status, Start/End Dates, Description, Type)
  • Historical Billable Mileage
  • Memorized Reports
  • Sales Tax Jurisdictions
✅
Audit Trails — Does Not Require RebuildingAudit Trails drop during migration, but IES has Audit Trails built in — they start working automatically. Pro Tip: Run an Audit Trail report in QBDT and save as PDF/Excel for future reference.

A. Labor Costing & Payroll

🚨
Timesheet & Pay Item Mappings Do Not MoveIf you use QuickBooks Time or Desktop timesheets, you must completely re-establish time-tracking connections and re-map pay items before the first payroll cycle. Previous mileage data does not transfer — manually add historical billable mileage for accurate client invoicing.

B. Project Timelines & Metadata

⚠️
Jobs → Projects (but Metadata is Stripped)Job Status, Start Date, Projected End Date, End Date, and Job Description/Type do not survive the transition. Project Managers must manually recreate these tracking details immediately post-migration. Use this as a strategic window to re-evaluate active projects and leverage multi-phase project architecture.

C. Subcontractor Compliance

🚨
Custom Compliance Fields Drop — Immediate RiskIf you rely on custom fields to track insurance expirations or contractor license numbers, you face an immediate risk of hiring an uninsured sub on Day 1. Vendor Custom Fields, Vendor Types, and Alternate Contacts all fall away. Compliance teams must manually rebuild these before authorizing any new site work.

D. Billing Clarity & Invoicing

  • Progress Invoicing Break: Active estimates are marked Closed; Inactive become Rejected. Estimates used for progress invoicing won't link to invoices from Desktop. Customers must reactivate estimates and relink invoices.
  • Custom Field Limits: "PO No" and "Sales Rep" safely move into Custom Fields. Complex custom fields (Project Manager, Contract Date) do not transfer directly.
  • The Subtotal Collapse: Subtotals do not move as individual line items — they aggregate into the Description field.

E. Banks & Sales Tax

  • Bank Feeds: Automation rules remain intact, but bank feed setup does not move. The Primary Admin must physically reconnect all bank accounts on Day 1.
  • Line Item Sales Tax: Not available in IES. If the customer needs this, they require a third-party AIA Billing application.
  • Sales Tax: Migrates as raw journal entries, often applying to wrong filings. Accounting must delete migrated entries and recreate them in the Sales Tax Center.

F. Historical Reporting

🚨
Historical Reports & Reconciliation Reports Do Not MoveBefore initiating migration, export and save all past reconciliation reports as PDFs. Any customized reporting packages (monthly client/departmental/project financials) must be entirely rebuilt from scratch.

🚀 QB Desktop → IES: What Changes for Retail & E-Commerce
Feature Area 📤 QB Desktop (From) ✅ IES (To) 💡 Strategic Outcome
Sales Sync Batch Connectors: Third-party apps sync sales once a day. Inventory counts lag, risking overselling. Unified Omnichannel: Real-time integration of sales channels. Inventory is committed across the entire portfolio as sales occur. Reliability: Prevent stockouts and overselling during high-volume periods.
Forecasting Manual Trends: Buyers guess reorder points based on historical bank feed patterns. AI-Driven Demand Planning: Predicts cash flow and inventory demand based on 5 years of history and seasonality. Cash Efficiency: Optimize stock levels so capital isn't tied up in slow-moving inventory.
Profitability by Channel Blended Margins: Hard to see true margins per channel (e.g., Amazon vs. Shopify) without manual tagging. Dimensional P&L: Slice profitability by Channel Dimension to see exactly which storefront yields the highest net margin. Strategy: Double down on the most profitable sales channels.
Inventory Valuations Concern: Fear of losing valuation methods or being forced into FIFO during cloud migration. Full support for Moving Average Cost (MAC) valuations. Item quantities and average costs are perfectly preserved during migration. Accurate inventory valuations and COGS without forcing your finance team to rework reporting methods.
Receiving & Order Fulfillment Disconnected purchase orders, receiving processes, and billing — manual reconciliation required. Item receipts separated from bills (partial or full receipt of goods). Enhanced Sales Orders connect directly to POs, estimates, and invoices. Inventory accuracy reflects real-time receipts — reducing accrual issues, duplicate payments, and manual reconciliation.

Scalability File Bloat: Large inventory lists and transaction histories slow down the system with performance lag. Cloud Scale: Architecture supports high-volume transactions and unlimited entities without performance degradation. Growth: Add new product lines or warehouses without worrying about crashing the file.
Payables & 3-Way Match Manual 3-Way Match: Staff manually compares Purchase Orders, Receiving Vouchers, and Vendor Bills. Automated Supply Chain: The Accounting AI automatically matches POs to Bills and Receipts, flagging discrepancies. Accuracy: Eliminate overpayments for goods not received.

Healthcare

Pre-migration questions for clinics, private practices, and multi-location healthcare providers.

1
Do you use a separate Practice Management Software (PMS) alongside QuickBooks?
↳ Layer 2: Are month-end journal entries your primary way of recording revenue from PMS into QuickBooks?
▾
📋 Feature & Migration Details
⚠ Integration Must Be Rebuilt

PMS Integration: Existing QuickBooks integrations with Practice Management Software do not carry over. API connections must be re-established post-migration for IES.

⚠️ Why It Matters

Revenue cycle data will stop flowing into IES until the PMS integration is rebuilt. This must be scoped and scheduled before migration to avoid a gap in revenue reporting on Day 1.

2
Do you track revenue separately by payer type (insurance vs. private pay)?
↳ Layer 2: Do you currently split revenue across multiple income accounts by payer or procedure type?
▾
📋 Feature & Migration Details
⚠ Manual Rebuild Required

Payer Dimensions: Revenue categorization by payer type must be reconfigured in IES using Dimensions (e.g., Insurance vs. Private Pay per clinic). Existing account structures migrate but tracking logic must be rebuilt.

⚠️ Why It Matters

Without correct payer dimensions, the practice cannot identify which procedures or payer types drive profitability — a critical metric for healthcare financial management.

3
Do you operate multiple clinic locations that share costs (e.g., medical supplies, admin staff)?
↳ Layer 2: Do you currently use manual journal entries to split shared costs across locations?
▾
📋 Feature & Migration Details
✗ Manual JEs Do Not Move

Cost Allocations: Manual allocation journal entries migrate as historical transactions but allocation logic does not. Cost allocation rules must be rebuilt in IES using AI Expense Agents.

⚠️ Why It Matters

Practices doing manual month-end math to split shared costs will need to re-establish this workflow in IES. The good news: IES can automate this going forward using revenue or asset % allocation.

4
Do you store or reference any patient health information (PHI) within QuickBooks?
↳ Layer 2: Do any third-party tools connected to QuickBooks process or transmit PHI?
▾
📋 Feature & Migration Details
⚠ Compliance Review Needed
